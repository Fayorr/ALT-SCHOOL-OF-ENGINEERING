Ofada Rice and Sauce

Ayamase Stew is a traditional Nigerian green pepper stew known for its distinctive smoky flavor and greenish color. Made with green bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, palm oil, locust beans (iru), and assorted meats or fish, it has a rich, spicy taste with earthy undertones. The stew gets its characteristic appearance from the blended green peppers and its bold flavor from the fermented locust beans. Often called "Designer Stew" in Lagos, Ayamase is typically served with ofada rice and is popular for its authentic, rustic taste that represents traditional Yoruba cuisine.

Recipe Information:

  • Prep time: 15 minutes
  • Cook time: 12 minutes
  • Servings: 20 Ladle soups
  • Difficulty: Beginner

Ingredients

  • 6 Green Bell Peppers (Green Tatashe)
  • 3-4 Scotch bonnet (Ata Rodo)
  • 1 Big Red Onion Chopped
  • Palm Oil
  • 1 Cup Chicken or Beef Stock (optional)
  • 4 Cups Boiled Assorted Meats - cut into bite size chunks
  • 2 Heaped Tablespoons Locust Beans (Iru)
  • 1 Cup Shredded Boiled Stockfish (Panla)
  • 2 Tablespoons Dried Crayfish
  • 2 knorr Chicken Cubes or your preferred bullion cube
  • Salt

Instructions:

  1. Place a big pot on a hob on medium heat, pour in palm oil, enough quantity to fry your meats. Leave to bleach for 15-10 minutes thereabouts, when the oil is bleached,
  2. Pour in your meats and fry till slightly crisp. When they’re all fried, scoop out the meat and side aside.
  3. Now you need at least a cup of Palm oil. Use the same Palm oil used for frying the meats. If you’ve got up to a cup, that’s fine if not, add more. Leave the pot on the hob.
  4. Add the onions. Sauté till fragrant…
  5. Now add the locust beans(Iru), sauté till it releases its flavour and aroma….
  6. Now add the crayfish, be careful not to add too much so it doesn’t overpower the authentic Ayamashe aroma, also add the shredded Stockfish…
  7. Fry for 2-3 minutes, then add the blended green pepper…
  8. Stir to combine, add the knorr cubes and salt to taste. Be careful not to add too much seasoning as you’ll be adding stock later on…
  9. Now cover and let this fry till oil floats to the top. Do check every now and then to prevent burning.
  10. When oil flows to the top, Now add the fried meat.
  11. You can add the stock if you find the consistency too thick as palm oil has a tendency to thicken soups up. Cook for a further 8-10 minutes.
  12. Switch off the heat and let it simmer with the residual heat, at this time, the oil will float to the top. And it’s done..
  13. Serve with Ofada rice or any kind of cooked white rice, fried plantain or even yam….

Tips to Note

  1. Bleaching Oil can be a dangerous, be careful!
  2. Use a fork to pierce your eggs in different locations, to allow the stew permeate into the egg.
